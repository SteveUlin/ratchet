## Why v3 (the motivating failure)

dream v2's greedy per-event router **over-merges**: on a real taro-project run it strengthened one
takeaway ("Zig structs are unnamed") with events about JAX autodiff and NumPy NEP-50, then graduated it
as "corroborated across 2 distinct sessions." Corroboration meant *co-occurrence in a takeaway*, not
*the same lesson recurring*. v3 makes corroboration structurally same-lesson.

# DREAM V3 — Statement-First Entity Resolution with Soft Subject Scope

Status: design proposal — **rev-3 final (2026-07-01)**. What the final fold changed, on measured data:
the identity architecture is stated honestly — deterministic REJECTION at $0, ONE bounded and audited
Haiku call owning ACCEPTANCE, a human audit at the gate (zero measured pairs reach the $0 merge bar, so
the old "mostly-deterministic identity" claim is dead); the shipped cascade is TWO bands, with
J_HIGH/J_CROSS kept as documented-dormant knobs; the claim pool gains a derived ACTIVE view (the drain);
`possibly-same` storage and the `distinct-from` edge kind are cut in favor of a derived merge-suggestion
query plus ONE compound `reject-merge` decision; CONTRADICTION_WEIGHT and the dual maturity bar move to
the backlog. Supersedes the dream-v2 greedy router (ADR-0010); extends the redesign arc
(ADR-0011/0012/0013–0019/0021–0024). Targets the motivating failure: v2 strengthened one takeaway from
three off-topic events (Zig struct naming + JAX autodiff + NumPy NEP-50) and graduated it as
"corroborated across 2 sessions." That is an **entity-resolution failure** — the LLM was asked to *pick*
which of N catalog claims an event belonged to, a forced choice biased toward merging. The cure is a
**statement-first, pairwise, deterministic-first** decision — rejection settled at $0, acceptance owned
by one bounded, abstention-default, match-keyed LLM call — with subject a **soft scope signal (never a
hard veto)**, and corroboration minted as an explicit, retractable, audited edge.

---

## 0. Thesis, stated honestly

Ratchet builds a **rough knowledge graph** by incremental view maintenance over the transcript stream.
v2's error had two parts: (a) corroboration identity was decided by an LLM doing **forced-choice over the
whole catalog** ("which of these N?"), which biases toward picking *something*; (b) "support" was mutated
in place, so a bad pick **latched** and could never be un-counted.

v3's identity architecture, in one line: **deterministic REJECTION at $0 + LLM-adjudicated ACCEPTANCE +
human audit at the gate.** Measured on the real store (284 events, 40,186 pairs), the deterministic
layer's whole job is throwing pairs away: 40,169 pairs sit below `J_MAYBE = 0.18` — non-match for free;
17 land in the residue; **zero** reach the $0 merge bar (`J_HIGH = 0.55` — the corpus maximum is 0.311,
and that pair is two DIFFERENT lessons sharing vocabulary, while genuine same-lesson paraphrases measure
0.21–0.24). On this corpus, ALL acceptance is LLM acceptance, and the design owns that: one bounded Haiku
call — comparative with an explicit none default, match-keyed for audit — owns the residue, and the
review gate renders exactly what it saw.

v3 fixes both v2 errors:

1. **Identity becomes a classified match, not a forced choice.** The resolver compares an event against
   candidate claims one pair at a time (record-linkage / Fellegi–Sunter). The deterministic signal
   settles NON-MATCH at $0 — the 40k mass; the **LLM owns acceptance, as ONE comparative call over the
   event's top-`K_RESIDUE` residue candidates with an explicit none option — none the stated default**
   (ComEM measured binary per-pair yes/no as the MOST over-merge-prone framing; comparative-with-none
   beats it, and abstention-by-default is Graphiti's production posture) — never the whole catalog, never
   a forced pick. Every merge it makes persists a **match key** the review card renders.

2. **Corroboration is a minted, retractable edge.** Support = `count(distinct sessions of corroborates
   edges into this claim)`, **recomputed on read** (ADR-0013). Withdraw an edge → the count decrements.
   It can never latch.

3. **Subject is a soft scope, not a gate.** Shared repo/file *raises* confidence in a match; disjoint
   subjects don't *forbid* one (that would kill cross-cutting principles like "use jj, not git" that
   recur across repos with no shared file) — they tag the claim **cross-cutting**, and the review card
   SHOWS the scope. One maturity bar: the dual bar is backlog — the measured corpus is single-project,
   so no cross-subject pair exists to tune a second bar against. This obeys the design-philosophy
   directive: explained, adjustable knobs with escape hatches, never hidden hard rules.

The op structure is documentation, not a new API: dream's single `route+apply` Block splits into a
deterministic-first **`resolve`** Block and a rare LLM **`synthesize`** Block. Everything else is today's
`block.Block` (ADR-0009) at its own hand-run cadence.

---

## 1. What actually changes (two Blocks, no new framework)

- **`resolve`** (new): carves out v2's `route`+`apply`. Statement-first cascade (§3): $0 rejection for
  the non-match mass, then ONE comparative-with-none Haiku call per event that lands residue candidates —
  on the measured corpus, 17 calls across the whole 40k-pair backlog. **Mints the claim node and the
  corroborates/contradicts edge immediately**, in-batch, so the next event in the same tick reads the
  claim it just created (read-your-writes, exactly as v2 folded new takeaways into the on-instance
  catalog — dream.py:52). New claims are born with a **provisional title (= the event summary) and
  `why = null`**.
- **`synthesize`** (rare, Sonnet): fills durable `title`/`why` prose. **Deferred — it fires only for a
  claim that has CROSSED the maturity bar** (or on review demand), never per-new-event (§7.3). An
  all-NEW cold-start corpus pays only the capped residue calls at resolve, and Sonnet only for lessons
  that actually recur.

Route-by-forced-choice is gone. Everything below (nodes, edges, confidence, view, review, backpressure)
is the surrounding model that makes those two Blocks correct and unstuck.

## 2. The knowledge-graph model (embedding-free)

Every node is a content-addressed immutable blob (ADR-0007); "the graph" is a **derived view** folded
from blobs + append-only decisions (ADR-0013 — recompute-on-read, store nothing a blob already implies).

### 2.1 Nodes

**EventNode** (L0, glean output — extended, no extra LLM call). Today's event gains two deterministic
feature arrays stamped at glean time:

```python
event["subject_key"] = {          # WHERE this lesson lives — the co-location scope, repo+file ONLY
    "repo":  str | None,          # concepts._repo_label(origin)  (cwd basename)
    "files": [str, ...],          # files WRITTEN, narrowed to the lesson's evidence span if derivable (§3.0)
    # NOTE: `tools` is DROPPED from the scope key — session_facts.tools are Claude Code tool names
    #       (Edit/Bash/Read/Grep), near-identical across every project, so they don't discriminate
    #       subjects (the v2-design's tools={zig}/tools={python} example was simply wrong: zig/python
    #       are Bash *arguments*, not tool names). Reuse session_facts, take files_edited + repo only.
}
event["stmt_sig"] = {             # a signature of the LESSON TEXT, tuned for SHORT text (no embeddings)
    "simhash":   uint64,          # SimHash over CHARACTER 4-shingles of the normalized summary
    "shingles":  [uint64, ...],   # the char-shingle set itself (exact Jaccard on candidates; feeds
                                  # the rare-shingle candidate index, §3.1)
    "entropy":   float,           # Shannon entropy of the normalized summary (triviality gate)
}
```

Why char-shingle SimHash, not word-3gram MinHash: a ~10-word summary has almost no *word* 3-grams, so
Graphiti's MinHash recipe (tuned for long episodic text) degenerates on it — and lexical-over-short-text
sparsity is **exactly what sank dream v1** (TF-IDF cosine over short quotes, ADR-0010 §Context). Character
shingles keep signal on short strings (SimHash / Charikar; used by search engines for short-doc near-dup).
We remain honest — and now measured: char-shingles are trusted for REJECTION only. In the 0.2–0.35 band
they cannot separate same from different (the corpus's top pair, 0.311, is two different lessons;
same-lesson paraphrases sit at 0.21–0.24), so the LLM owns all acceptance (§3).

**ClaimNode** (= v2's takeaway, renamed for what it is — the L1 rough-graph node). The blob stores **seed
identity only**: `{id, title, why, relation, seed_event, born}`. Everything evidential — `support`,
`contradictions`, `evidence`, sessions, `last_seen`, recency-weighted entrenchment (ADR-0023), and the
attributes below — **derives at fold time from live edges + event blobs** (ADR-0013: store nothing a blob
already implies). That storage discipline is the precondition for split-by-retraction (§2.2):

```python
claim.subject_key = union of its corroborating events' subject_keys       # recomputed from edges
claim.stmt_sig    = signature over the SEED summary ∪ corroborating events' summaries
                    # a fold over LIVE edges — retraction shrinks the merge basin automatically.
                    # NEVER over synthesized title/why prose: synthesis jumps register (event
                    # summaries are concrete, Sonnet prose is general), and signing the prose
                    # chains transitive generalization — each merge widens the signature, which
                    # widens the next merge.
claim.scope       = "local" if evidence shares a file/repo else "cross-cutting"   # display, §3.4
claim.why         = str | None   # None until synthesize runs (§7.3)
```

**The ACTIVE pool is a derived view, not a stored status.** `active = claims whose recency-weighted
entrenchment >= ACTIVE_FLOOR, or with evidence within ACTIVE_DAYS` (named knobs). Claims outside the view
fold out of the subject/statement indexes and out of candidate generation; their blobs, edges, and history
remain, and a re-seeded near-duplicate reconciles later through the derived merge-suggestion query at
review (§6). The view exists because it is the pool's only drain: v3 mints a claim on every zero-match
event, and `forget` — which drained v2's pool by evicting un-consolidated EVENTS — never fires on the
main path, because resolve consolidates every event it sees. Without the view the claim pool grows
monotonically, and the candidate indexes with it.

`state` (FSRS D/S/R) from the prior draft is **dropped** — see §4.

**ConceptNode** (reviewed) — unchanged. A concept is not a separate graph; it is a ClaimNode elevated by
an accept **facet** (§5).

### 2.2 Edges — all explicit, none by embedding similarity

| Edge | From → To | Meaning | Minted by | Match key on edge |
|------|-----------|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **corroborates** | event → claim | +1 support | `resolve` ONLY | `{stmt_sim, subj, by, candidates_shown, prompt_version, model}` |
| **contradicts** | event → claim | +1 contradiction (ADR-0012) | `resolve` | same |
| cites / derived-from | claim → event → cleaned → raw | provenance byte-span | synth / weave | — |
| shares-file/repo | claim ↔ claim | facet overlap (rebuildable) | `concepts.derived_edges` | shared members |
| generalizes/supersedes/relates-to | claim ↔ claim | asserted structure | gardener (ADR-0015) | note |
| accept/reject/snooze/retire | decision → claim | review-status facet (§5) | REVIEW | reviewer + assessment |
| **reject-merge** | decision → {edge_id \| pair, event_id} | retraction + reopen + pair-block, ONE blob | REVIEW only | reviewer + rationale |

The **corroborates edge is the whole fix.** In v2 support was mutated in place; in v3 it is a signed
aggregate over edges (DBSP / differential-dataflow retraction-correct counting), recomputed on read. The
match key is an **audit record**: `{stmt_sim, subj, by, candidates_shown: [claim_ids], prompt_version,
model}` — from the edge alone one can reconstruct what the model saw and re-score its verdict against a
gold set later. `by` records `"llm"` (the residue call) or `"det"` — on the shipped two-band path every
merge is `by:"llm"`; the `det` value exists for the dormant bands (§3.1).

**Split is edge-retraction (Senzing "unresolve") — named so it stays testable.** Because the claim blob
stores seed identity only (§2.1) and every evidential attribute folds from live edges, retracting a
corroborates edge IS the split: support decrements, scope, subject_key, and stmt_sig recompute, the
evidence entry leaves the fold — no rewrite, no migration, no residue on the blob. The testable property:
fold(claim after retraction) == fold(claim had the event never matched).

**The human "not the same" verdict is ONE compound decision** — verb `reject-merge`, carrying
`{edge_id | pair, event_id}`. Three folds read the same blob: the edge fold reads it as retraction, the
working-set fold reads it as reopen (the event re-enters the resolvable pool and seeds its own claim next
tick), and resolve's candidate filter reads it as a permanent pair-block. One append, atomic, no crash
windows — the previous retract→reopen→mint-distinct-from triple-append had two: die after the retract and
the event is silently stranded; die before the block-edge and the next tick RE-FORMS the torn-out merge
via latest-wins. Resolve's done-marker key carries an **epoch** — the count of reject-merge decisions
naming the event — so a reopened event's key is new and the Block driver's per-item done-index (block.py)
does not skip it forever.

**Merge suggestions are a derived query, not storage** (rendered in §6): pairs of live ACTIVE claims
whose stmt_sigs land in the residue band — a pure function of stored signatures — minus reject-merge'd
pairs, computed at review-render time, capped per card, and folded out after `SUGGEST_TTL_DAYS` without
new evidence (view-level knobs, zero stored state). Nothing can harden: Wikidata's P460 failure —
unattended soft links becoming de-facto merges — has no stored object to happen to.

## 3. `resolve` — statement-first cascade, subject as soft scope

`resolve.process(event)` replaces `route`+`apply`. Statement identity is primary; subject modulates
candidacy and scope display, and never vetoes.

### 3.0 Narrow the subject to the lesson, not the session

`session_facts(active_path)` unions *every* file written in the whole session (concepts.py) — a 15-file
refactor stamps all 15 onto every event, and two unrelated lessons from one session get an identical
subject_key. Before stamping, **narrow `files` to the lesson's own evidence span**: weave already renders
a `→ Edit: /path` line per write into the cleaned blob, so co-location is a deterministic byte-window scan
around the event's cited span (`evidence[].span`) — no new data, no heuristics. Fall back to the session
union when no `→ Edit:` line lands in the window.

Measured (2026-07-01, `subject.py`): the mechanism is real but **mostly dormant** — 47% of cleaned blobs
contain no write-tool line at all (discussion/read-only sessions → empty subject_key, seed-only per §3.1
step 0), and within write-having blobs a ±4000-byte window (`SPAN_WINDOW`) covers a mean ~6% of blob
bytes, so the session-union fallback dominates. Narrowing stays as a cheap, already-built precision aid,
not a load-bearing defense. The fallback's cost is bounded — statement similarity is the primary key, and
maturity counts distinct sessions, so same-session mutual candidacy cannot fake-mature a claim — it just
widens the candidate net. **`J_MAYBE`, not `SPAN_WINDOW`, is the first knob a gold set should tune.**

### 3.1 The cascade (two bands shipped)

```
0. NORMALIZE + TRIVIALITY GATE (pairwise): a pair may MERGE only if
   min(entropy(event), entropy(claim.statement)) >= H_MIN — a low-signal statement on EITHER side
   is not evidence of "same lesson"; a sub-H_MIN event may still SEED. Measured: 0 of 284 events
   sit under H_MIN=3.0 — the gate is currently dormant, kept because it costs one comparison.
   EMPTY subject_key (discussion / no-edit session, no cwd) is SEED-only via subject: it reaches a
   claim only through the statement index, never through empty∩empty "overlap".

1. CANDIDATE GENERATION (blocking, recall-first — the UNION, not the intersection):
       C_subject   = claims sharing a repo/file facet   (subject index over the ACTIVE view, §3.3)
       C_statement = claims sharing >= RARE_MIN of the event's K_RARE RAREST shingles
                     (inverted index over the active pool; rarity = document frequency, recomputed
                     per tick; K_RARE = 8, RARE_MIN = 2 — named, UNTUNED knobs)
       candidates  = (C_subject ∪ C_statement) minus pairs with a live reject-merge verdict (§2.2)
   The statement channel is RARE SHINGLES, not SimHash-LSH, because measurement says LSH cannot do
   this job: the 4×16-bit bands collide only for near-duplicates (J ≳ 0.6), while real same-lesson
   paraphrases sit at 0.21–0.24 — the LSH channel never surfaces them. And with the repo facet
   devalued (below) and 47% of events carrying no writes, subject candidacy is thin too: on the
   measured corpus most true pairs would never even be COMPARED. Two tellings of one lesson share
   its unusual words even at 0.2 overall similarity, so an inverted index over each statement's
   rarest shingles reaches down to residue-band similarities where the matches actually live —
   stdlib dict, O(active pool) build like the other indexes. LSH-band collision may stay as a cheap
   exact-near-dup fast path, but it provides no paraphrase recall and the design claims none.
   A facet appearing on more than FACET_DF_MAX of active claims contributes 0 to subject candidacy
   and overlap — the one-line deterministic slice of Senzing's shared-feature devaluation: on a
   single-repo corpus the repo facet links everything to everything; devaluing it keeps the subject
   index discriminative, while the statement channel still recalls cross-cutting lessons, so
   nothing is vetoed. The UNION keeps those lessons alive: "use jj, not git" recurring in a NEW
   repo shares no file with the existing claim, but its statement's rare shingles collide → still a
   candidate. (Canopy clustering, McCallum/Nigam/Ungar 2000: cheap-distance canopy → expensive
   within-canopy compare.)

2. PAIRWISE CLASSIFY (deterministic, $0 — the REJECTION layer):
       stmt_sim = Jaccard over char-shingles (exact, on the small candidate set)
       stmt_sim <  J_MAYBE   -> NON-MATCH   (measured: 40,169 of 40,186 pairs — the free mass)
       stmt_sim >= J_MAYBE   -> RESIDUE     (measured: 17 pairs corpus-wide)

2b. RESIDUE ADJUDICATION (the ACCEPTANCE layer — fires when step 2 yields >= 1 residue candidate):
       ONE Haiku call over the event's top-K_RESIDUE residue candidates by stmt_sim (K_RESIDUE a
       named knob, default 3; overflow candidates are simply not shown — the abstention posture is
       preserved: an unseen candidate defaults to no-merge). Comparative framing with an EXPLICIT
       none option, none stated as the expected default ("most events are new lessons; pick a
       candidate only if it teaches the SAME lesson"). Never per-pair yes/no (ComEM: the MOST
       over-merge-prone framing), never the whole catalog (v2).
         picks candidate k   -> corroborates(event -> k, by:"llm"), match key on the edge
         CONTRADICTS k       -> contradicts(event -> k) — the negation cue, ADR-0012 WEAKEN
         none / unparseable  -> mint a new claim (the default)

3. RESOLVE:
       corroborates / contradicts as verdicted; zero candidates or a none verdict -> MINT a new
       ClaimNode NOW (deterministic id = mint_takeaway_id(event.id), title = event.summary,
       why = null) + corroborates(event -> it).
```

**Dormant knobs.** `J_HIGH = 0.55` (deterministic merge at shared subject) and `J_CROSS = 0.70` (disjoint
subject) remain in `classify()` (sig.py) but no measured pair reaches them — the corpus maximum is 0.311.
They are DOCUMENTED-DORMANT, and reactivating them requires a **negation guard** first: a polarity-flipped
restatement ("always X" / "never X") scores Jaccard ≈ 0.73 and would count as $0 support (backlog:
`NEGATION_TOKENS`, §9). The `match-cross` band leaves the shipped path with them.

Steps 2–3 (2b included) commit per event (ADR-0009) and the minted claim/edge is visible to the rest of
the batch — no lagging synthesize step in the identity path, so the duplicate-seed race is closed.

### 3.2 Why this kills the exact failure

The three v2 events (Zig struct naming / JAX autodiff / NumPy NEP-50) have **different statements** —
their pairwise sims sit far below `J_MAYBE` (verified on the real store), so every pair is NON-MATCH
structurally, regardless of subject. Each seeds its own claim. And where the LLM does own the call — the
residue, which on the measured corpus is ALL acceptance — it sees a small pre-filtered candidate set with
an explicit none default (ComEM: comparative-with-none is the least over-merge-prone framing; v2's forced
choice over the whole catalog had no none at all), an unparseable answer is a no-merge, and every merge it
makes carries a match key the review card renders (§6). The spurious "2 sessions" cannot form: support
counts distinct-session corroborates edges, and there are none.

### 3.3 The indexes — bounded by the ACTIVE view

The subject index (`{repo/file -> [claim_ids]}`) and the rare-shingle statement index (`{shingle ->
[claim_ids]}`, §3.1) rebuild from the **ACTIVE claim view only** (§2.1) — not the whole store. The view
is the bound: claims fold out of it by dormancy
(`ACTIVE_FLOOR`/`ACTIVE_DAYS`), mature/accepted claims live in the trusted view (§5), a separate,
review-bounded index. Per-tick index build is O(active claims) + O(trusted); per-event query is
O(candidates-in-block). Neither is ∝ raw transcript store. (LightRAG local-graph merge vs nano-graphrag
whole-graph refresh; DBSP incremental maintenance.)

### 3.4 Scope: shown, not wired

`claim.scope` recomputes from its corroborates edges' subject_keys: **`local`** if the evidence shares a
file or repo, **`cross-cutting`** if it spans ≥2 repos with no shared file. One maturity bar
(`MATURITY_WEIGHT` — today's, unchanged); scope is **shown on the review card**, not wired to a second
bar. The dual bar (`MATURITY_WEIGHT_CROSS`) is backlog (§9): the measured corpus is single-project, so
no cross-subject pair exists to tune it against, and it is a one-line fold-time addition the day one does.

All shipped thresholds — `H_MIN`, `J_MAYBE`, `K_RARE`/`RARE_MIN`, `K_RESIDUE`, `FACET_DF_MAX`,
`SPAN_WINDOW` (§3.0),
`ACTIVE_FLOOR`/`ACTIVE_DAYS` (§2.1), `SUGGEST_TTL_DAYS` (§2.2), `MATURITY_WEIGHT`, `FORGET_MIN_DAYS`
(§7.3), the subject facet weights (`W_FILE`/`W_REPO`) — are named, explained, CLI-overridable knobs (the
design-philosophy directive). `J_HIGH`/`J_CROSS` are documented-dormant (§3.1); `tools` weight is fixed
at 0 (non-discriminative, per §2.1).

## 4. Confidence: recency-weighted distinct sessions (FSRS rejected)

Keep exactly what ratchet already has and drop the FSRS import from the prior draft. The maturity gate is
already `net_entrenchment >= bar` (dream.py) — recency-weighted (`recency_weight = 0.5**(age/halflife)`,
ADR-0023) distinct-session entrenchment, which by construction makes **recurrence beat co-occurrence**:
three quotes in one session are one distinct session (barely moves the bar), the same lesson weeks later
is a second fresh session (moves it hard). This is *already* the continuous form the FSRS draft reached
for, and it has two properties FSRS breaks:

- **Recompute-on-read (ADR-0013).** `net_entrenchment` recomputes from evidence session-ids + valid-times
  every read; nothing is stored on the claim. FSRS's `{D,S,R,last}` would have to be *stored* on the blob
  and *mutated* per event.
- **Retraction-correctness (§2.2).** Support is an aggregate over edges — withdraw an edge, recompute,
  it drops. FSRS's Stability update is *path-dependent*: you cannot un-apply one edge without replaying
  the whole update sequence, which silently breaks the retraction guarantee the edge model exists to give.

The staleness signal the FSRS decay term promised is already present: `recency_weight` decaying toward 0
*is* the "quiet claim needs re-validation" signal that feeds the `decay` pass (ADR-0024). No new model.

Contradictions stay symmetric for now: `net_entrenchment = Σ w(support sessions) − Σ w(contradiction
sessions)`, exactly ADR-0012's additive twin. `CONTRADICTION_WEIGHT` (a fold-time coefficient making
beliefs fall faster than they rise) and Knowledge-Vault sqrt saturation on the support side are both
**backlog** (§9) — the same class: speculative arithmetic awaiting live contested / cross-cutting data,
each a one-line fold-time addition the day evidence arrives. The corpus has produced no contested claim
to tune either against.

## 5. One graph, one derived high-confidence view

**One append-only graph. Human review appends an accept/status *facet*; the "high-confidence view" is a
derived query used by refine + generate. No second graph.** Ratchet already is this and it's where every
serious system lands (Argilla suggestion-vs-response on one record; Graphiti bi-temporal
invalidate-don't-delete; DBSP/Materialize base-collection + materialized views; MemGPT core-vs-archival;
basic-memory/Logseq saved-query views).

```python
def high_confidence_view(root) -> list[ClaimNode]:
    """The trusted subset — the ONLY thing generate reads, and refine's 'what we already know' prior.
       ONE graph, filtered:  latest_decision == accept
                             AND net_entrenchment >= MATURITY_WEIGHT
                             AND no live retire/supersede (bi-temporal invalidate-don't-delete)."""
```

- **Refine** reads the view as its trusted prior (today's `concept_digest`) for belief-change, but
  `resolve` runs new events against the *active* rough pool (rough claims still accrue evidence).
- **Generate** reads *only* the view (unchanged, ADR-0020).
- **Review flowing back** = appending a decision facet, shifting a ClaimNode rough → trusted *in place*.
  Single-source lineage, no migration, decision-blob timeline preserved (ADR-0007). A rejected claim
  stays labeled-and-invisible and suppresses near-dupes (MemPrompt / ADR-0008), so a dismissed lesson
  isn't re-surfaced. A second post-review graph would duplicate lineage and discard the decision blobs
  that drive every derived view — strictly worse, rejected.

## 6. Human review — validation AND a teaching surface

Review is the trust boundary (ADR-0008) and stays a human gate. `review.pending` already sorts by
importance (`net_entrenchment × confidence`) and shows the maturity `rationale` (ADR-0022/0027). That
ordering suffices: the queue is a sitting-sized list, and a surprise re-ranking layer is cut as machinery
without a measured need.

What the reviewer is shown per claim:
1. The `why` (untrusted) + **verified byte-span quotes** (existing `resolve_evidence`).
2. **The corroboration story:** "seen in session A (2026-06-12, repo `ratchet`, file `dream.py`) and again
   in session C (2026-06-27, same file) — recurred after 15 days." The recurrence narrative + the
   subject_key that scoped it (and the claim's `scope` tag, §3.4) teaches *the pattern in his work*, not
   just a rule.
3. **The LLM-merge audit card (the v2-failure detector):** for any claim whose maturity rests on
   `by:"llm"` edges — on the shipped two-band path, **every merged claim** — the card renders each
   corroboration's verified quote alongside its match key (stmt_sim, candidates_shown, model), and a ⚠
   line marks disjoint-subject evidence. This replaces the old banner predicate (disjoint AND llm), which
   pointed at the wrong door: same-subject LLM merges were invisible to it, and same-subject is the
   dominant measured case. The reviewer audits the acceptance layer with the same evidence the model saw
   — this card would have caught the motivating failure at the gate. (Legacy v2 claims carry no match
   keys — moot: the v2 reset retires every v2 takeaway and reopens its events, so every claim the card
   sees is v3-minted.)
4. **Delta to the trusted view:** "accepting this retires concept `c-abc` (you moved on from X)" — the
   ADR-0024 staleness/contradiction narrative.
5. **Merge suggestions (derived, §2.2):** residue-band pairs among live claims render as the two claims'
   QUOTES side by side — not the stmt_sim number, which in the 0.2–0.35 band is noise — capped per card,
   expiring after `SUGGEST_TTL_DAYS` without new evidence. Confirm → the existing `dream.merge` machinery
   (edge re-pointing under v3) — NOT the tier-2 gardener gate, which applies CONCEPT ops; claims have no
   path through it. Dismiss → `reject-merge`, never asked again.
6. **Contested claims surface:** a rough claim within one session of the bar that carries a live
   contradicts edge gets a review card (v2's `contradicted_takeaways` visibility, carried forward) — a
   wrong llm CONTRADICTS verdict must not silently suppress a good claim.

**Negative verdicts persist** as the ONE compound `reject-merge` decision (§2.2): retraction + reopen +
permanent pair-block in a single atomic append. Only review mints it — a permanent negative constraint is
a trust-boundary artifact (ADR-0008), never a machine's call.

**Review never blocks on another Block's cadence.** A mature-but-unsynthesized claim APPEARS in pending
with its provisional title and a "why pending" badge; the review CLI/skill invokes `synthesize` on demand
for the surfaced sitting (§7.3). The loop must close for a monthly operator — a matured claim must never
sit invisible waiting for a hand-run Sonnet tick.

**Queue sizing stays "a sitting's worth."** The fatigue data grounds the default: defect detection
collapses past ~60–90 minutes or ~400 review units per sitting (SmartBear/Cisco), noisy queues die
outright (Wikidata Primary Sources never drained), and per-item scrutiny beats batch-accept but drifts
toward accept-all under volume (Buçinca CSCW 2021). The default `--limit` keeps a sitting far below those
ceilings; the audit card + corroboration story keep per-item cost low; merge suggestions ride existing
cards instead of adding queue items. Small and high-signal, or the gate stops gating.

Two tiers stay (ADR-0017): tier-1 promotes ClaimNodes to concepts; tier-2 gates the gardener's structural
CONCEPT ops. Both append decision facets; neither forks a graph.

## 7. No stuck stages — grounded in the backpressure ratchet already has

### 7.1 Failure modes forbidden
- v1's whole-store re-cluster per run (O(store)/tick → stuck at scale).
- v2's per-event LLM route against the whole in-prompt catalog (cost ∝ catalog size; ingest cadence
  coupled to synthesis cadence → the LLM step throttles the whole pipeline).

### 7.2 The guarantees (block.py + recompute-on-read, costs owned)
1. **Bounded ticks, honest floor.** `resolve` touches only the delta (un-consolidated events) against the
   subject/statement indexes over the *ACTIVE view* (§3.3), never the whole graph, and the catalog no
   longer rides in any prompt. One cost is owned plainly: recompute-on-read makes each tick **O(lifetime
   writes) at the fold layer** — the done-index scan, the decision scans, retracted edges revisited on
   every fold (block.py `done_index`, the dream-style decision walks). At personal scale — hundreds of
   events, thousands of decisions — that is fine, and stays fine for years. Two relief valves are named
   and deferred until it bites: a one-time re-stamp of the 284 legacy events as new event versions
   (collapses pre-v3 marker history), and a content-addressed fold-snapshot cache.
2. **Budget = backpressure, and paid work never starves free work.** `--limit`/`--max-usd` already cap
   each Block tick, committing per item (block.py). resolve refines the driver's plain break-on-budget:
   when a tick's `--max-usd` is exhausted, residue-band events are **deferred** — no verdict, no marker,
   they retry next tick — while $0-path events continue to completion. The tick never breaks mid-list
   because a paid call starved free work. Resolve's Report surfaces the residue-call count and the
   deferrals; the RUNBOOK row carries a small default `--max-usd` (§8). One trend is owned plainly too:
   as the active pool grows, P(an event lands ≥1 residue candidate) → 1, so resolve tends toward **one
   Haiku call per event**. The dollars stay trivial; the real cost is serial `claude -p` wall-clock under
   rate-limit ride-out — exactly what the deferral rule and the per-tick budget bound.
3. **Crash-safety unchanged.** Per-item commit + processed-marker-last (ADR-0009); deterministic-keyed
   blobs and epoch-keyed markers for reopened events (§2.2), so a retried tick no-ops and a reopened
   event re-enters.

### 7.3 synthesize + forget: matured-only, fingerprinted, eviction-safe

The prior draft made `synthesize` fire per-new-claim (Sonnet); rev-3 moved it to "within one session of
the bar." Both re-create Sonnet-per-event on a cold corpus — at bar 1.5 a fresh one-session seed sits 0.5
away, i.e. within one session, so nearly every seed qualifies. Final:

- **Trigger: `net_entrenchment >= bar`** — synthesize the matured, just before review — or explicit
  review demand (§6). A never-recurring lesson is never synthesized; the synth queue is bounded by the
  graduation rate, not the new-event rate.
- **Idempotency marker keys on `(claim_id, prompt_version, model)`**, so decay or re-crossing the bar
  cannot re-pay the call; re-synthesis is review-demand only.
- **Synthesize stamps the live corroborates-edge-set fingerprint it consumed.** The fold flags
  `why-stale` when the live edge set diverges — a retraction after synthesis otherwise leaves fused prose
  latched. The `why` is the one stored non-derived field, so it is the one place staleness must be
  detected rather than recomputed away.
- Optional legibility knob: a $0.001 Haiku one-liner `why` at seed, upgraded to Sonnet at maturity.
  Default off (provisional title = event summary is enough for the rough graph).

**forget** stays the conservative straggler eviction (never age alone — ADR-0010 §6), now scoped to the
events that can still linger (budget-deferred residue, reopened events), with two amendments for v3's
cheap ticks: residency counts only **resolve-stage runs postdating `max(event.fetched_at, latest
reject-merge reopen)`**, and a wall-clock **`FORGET_MIN_DAYS`** accompanies the cycle count. Frequent
cheap ticks must not accelerate eviction, and a reopened event must not be mass-staled at its first
finalize because pre-reopen runs counted against it. Migration note (RUNBOOK): the v2-reset drain runs
with forget disabled (`--no-forget`) until pending hits 0 — a freshly reopened backlog is all
"stragglers" by cycle count, and must not be staled mid-drain.

The Aging strategy (ADR-0021) guarantees the deferred residue tail drains under sustained budget. Net:
every queue is bounded, every tick amortized-bounded, and the paid path defers instead of blocking, so
the LLM never throttles ingest.

## Key decisions

### 1. How do we fix the over-merge: tune the clustering threshold, or change the mechanism?

**Recommendation:** Change the mechanism to a statement-FIRST pairwise resolver: deterministic REJECTION at $0, ONE comparative-with-none Haiku call owning ACCEPTANCE over a pre-filtered top-K_RESIDUE handful, human audit at the gate. Subject overlap is a soft candidacy signal, not a gate. Corroboration is a minted, match-keyed edge.

**Why:** The v2 failure (Zig+JAX+NEP-50) was forced-choice-over-catalog, which biases toward picking something. Making statement the primary key blocks it structurally: the three events' pairwise sims sit far below J_MAYBE, so they are non-match regardless of subject (verified). And we say honestly what measurement showed: zero pairs reach the $0 merge bar, so on this corpus ALL acceptance is the LLM's — bounded to a pre-filtered handful with an explicit none default, match-keyed for audit. Deterministic-first, not "mostly deterministic."

**Prior art:** Fellegi–Sunter record linkage / Splink / Dedupe.io (match/possible/non-match); Canopy clustering (McCallum/Nigam/Ungar 2000); SimHash/Charikar for short-text near-dup; ComEM (comparative-with-none beats per-pair yes/no); ratchet's own dream-v1 post-mortem (lexical-over-short-text sparsity, ADR-0010).
### 2. Should disjoint subjects HARD-VETO a corroboration, or soft-scope it — and does scope need its own maturity bar?

**Recommendation:** Soft-scope, single bar. Subject overlap aids candidacy; disjoint subjects never forbid a match — they tag the claim `cross-cutting`, and the review card SHOWS the scope. Candidate generation is the UNION of subject-facet and rare-shingle statement neighbors. The dual bar (MATURITY_WEIGHT_CROSS) moves to the backlog.

**Why:** A hard subject veto structurally forbids cross-cutting principles (jj-not-git, workflow rules) from EVER maturing — exactly what a global CLAUDE.md is for — and violates the design-philosophy directive. But the stricter second bar is speculative arithmetic: the measured corpus is single-project, so the cross bands are unexercised and there is no data to tune a second bar against. Show scope to the human now; wire arithmetic to it the day a multi-project corpus produces evidence.

**Prior art:** neo4j-graphrag scoping as a recall aid not a filter; the design-philosophy directive (ADR-0025/0026/0027); faceted specificity classification (project-local vs cross-cutting).
### 3. Split resolve and synthesize into two Blocks — does that create a stale-read / duplicate-seed race?

**Recommendation:** Split them, but have `resolve` MINT the claim node and corroborates edge IMMEDIATELY (deterministic id from the seed event, title=event summary, why=null), in-batch. `synthesize` only fills prose later, and fires only for a claim that has CROSSED the maturity bar (or on review demand).

**Why:** The prior draft's eager-resolve/lagging-synthesize split reintroduced under-merge: within one resolve batch two events for the same brand-new lesson both find zero survivors and both seed, because the claim isn't minted until synthesize runs. Minting at resolve time restores v2's within-batch read-your-writes (dream.py:52). Deferring synthesize to matured claims kills the cold-start cost blow-up: an all-NEW early corpus pays Sonnet only for lessons that actually recur, so the synth queue is bounded by graduation rate, not new-event rate.

**Prior art:** Read-your-writes / streaming-join consistency (Kafka Streams, Flink watermarks); Generative Agents importance-triggered (not per-event) reflection; Mem0 accumulate-then-resolve.
### 4. Model confidence with FSRS (Difficulty/Stability/Retrievability), or keep recency-weighted distinct-session entrenchment?

**Recommendation:** Keep recency-weighted distinct-session net_entrenchment (ADR-0023). Drop FSRS entirely.

**Why:** The maturity gate is ALREADY net_entrenchment >= bar with recency_weight = 0.5**(age/halflife) — already the continuous 'recurrence beats co-occurrence' form (three quotes in one session = one distinct session; the same lesson weeks later = a second fresh session). FSRS adds untuned stateful params AND breaks two invariants this very design depends on: it stores {D,S,R,last} ON the claim blob (violates recompute-on-read, ADR-0013) and its path-dependent Stability update breaks retraction-correctness (you cannot un-apply one withdrawn edge without replaying the whole sequence). The decay signal FSRS promised is already present as recency_weight feeding the decay pass (ADR-0024).

**Prior art:** DBSP/differential-dataflow retraction-correct aggregation (support as a signed edge count, recomputed on read); ADR-0013 recompute-on-read discipline; ADR-0023 recency weighting already shipped.
### 5. One graph with a derived high-confidence view, or a separate post-review graph?

**Recommendation:** One append-only graph. Review appends an accept/status decision FACET; high_confidence_view is a derived query (accepted AND matured AND not retired) read by refine and generate.

**Why:** Ratchet already is this: review writes append-only decision blobs and the trusted set is a derived fold (ADR-0007/0008). A second graph duplicates lineage and discards the decision blobs that drive every view. One graph keeps provenance single-source, lets a rejected claim stay labeled-and-suppressible (near-dupe suppression, MemPrompt/ADR-0008), and makes review a delta into the same store.

**Prior art:** Argilla suggestion-vs-response on one record; Graphiti bi-temporal invalidate-don't-delete; DBSP/Materialize base-collection + materialized views; MemGPT core-vs-archival; basic-memory/Logseq saved-query views.
### 6. Do we need the Op/Cadence typed API and Reactive-Streams backpressure framing?

**Recommendation:** No. Demote both to documentation. Keep the INGEST/REFINE/REVIEW/PRODUCE grouping and per-Block cadence as a table in RUNBOOK.md; the only real code change is the resolve/synthesize Block split. Backpressure stays the existing --limit/--max-usd per-tick caps + Report.pending, with resolve deferring paid residue calls rather than breaking mid-list (§7.2).

**Why:** The prior draft declared frozen Op/Cadence/Substage dataclasses with on_cron/on_upstream_delta triggers, then admitted there is no orchestrator reading them — a Dagster-shaped API with no scheduler, over a hand-run CLI. And 'Reactive Streams request(n)' narrated machinery that block.py already provides. A doc table suffices until a runtime consumes it.

**Prior art:** block.py --limit/--max-usd + Report.pending (already shipped); RUNBOOK.md prose cadence; the design-philosophy preference for the smallest mechanism that carries the principle.
### 7. Residue framing: per-pair yes/no, or one comparative call with an explicit none?

**Recommendation:** One Haiku call per event over its top-K_RESIDUE residue candidates by stmt_sim (K_RESIDUE default 3; overflow candidates simply not shown): comparative framing, an explicit none option, none stated as the expected default. Verdicts: pick k → corroborates(by:llm); contradicts k → contradicts edge; none/unparseable → mint a new claim. On the shipped two-band cascade this call is the ONLY acceptance path.

**Why:** Measured across 8 datasets and 10 LLMs, binary pairwise yes/no is the MOST over-merge-prone matching framing — an isolated pair lacks the contrast that reveals 'none of these,' so the model leans yes. Selecting from a small candidate set WITH an explicit none beats it, and costs one call instead of k. The none-is-expected default is the abstention bias Graphiti ships in production. Capping at K_RESIDUE preserves the posture: an unshown candidate defaults to no-merge, never to a forced comparison.

**Prior art:** ComEM (arXiv 2405.16884 — select-with-none beats pairwise boolean); Graphiti's production dedupe prompt (abstention default); Fellegi–Sunter bands upstream.
### 8. Who adjudicates the residue band — the LLM, or the human clerical-review queue?

**Recommendation:** The LLM, with abstention the default; the human audits asynchronously at the gate, never through a blocking micro-verdict queue. Every LLM merge renders on the review card with its verified quotes and match key (§6.3); every borderline non-merge that leaves two similar live claims re-surfaces through the DERIVED merge-suggestion query (§2.2) — computed at render time from stored stmt_sigs, capped per card, expiring after SUGGEST_TTL_DAYS, zero stored suggestion state.

**Why:** Fellegi–Sunter routes the middle band to human clerical review, and ratchet already owns a human gate — but a blocking micro-verdict queue is how gates die: noisy queues never drain (Wikidata Primary Sources), and forced per-item scrutiny drifts toward accept-all under volume (Buçinca 2021). So the clerical band is relocated, not deleted: the human sees both sides of every borderline call attached to cards already under review. Deriving suggestions instead of storing them removes the hardening failure by construction — Wikidata's P460 lesson (unattended soft links become de-facto merges) has no stored object to happen to.

**Prior art:** Fellegi–Sunter three-way decision; Wikidata P460 + its documented hardening failure; Wikidata Primary Sources post-mortem; Buçinca CSCW 2021; SmartBear/Cisco review-fatigue data.
### 9. How does a human "not the same" verdict persist?

**Recommendation:** ONE compound decision blob, verb `reject-merge`, carrying {edge_id | pair, event_id}, minted by REVIEW only. Three folds read the same blob: the edge fold as retraction, the working-set fold as reopen, resolve's candidate filter as a permanent pair-block. Resolve's done-marker key carries an epoch (count of reject-merge decisions naming the event) so the reopened event re-enters the driver's item set. No `distinct-from` edge kind; no triple-append.

**Why:** The previous design (retract decision + reopen + mint distinct-from) had two crash windows: die after the retract and the event is silently stranded; die before the block-edge and the next tick re-forms the torn-out merge via latest-wins — resolve's blobs are deterministic-keyed, so same signatures, same verdict, same edge. One atomic append closes both, and the epoch closes the third gap: per-item done markers would otherwise skip a reopened event forever. Review-only minting keeps permanent negative constraints inside the trust boundary (ADR-0008); the decision is append-only and retractable like every other — the design-philosophy escape hatch.

**Prior art:** Wikidata P1889 "different from" (pair-level negative constraint recorded once at human-review time — here as a decision, not an edge kind); Senzing reversible-resolution discipline; ADR-0008.
### 10. Corroboration arithmetic: keep the linear symmetric net, or shape it?

**Recommendation:** Keep it linear and symmetric: `net = Σ w(support sessions) − Σ w(contradiction sessions)`, one MATURITY_WEIGHT bar. CONTRADICTION_WEIGHT and sqrt saturation both go to the backlog (§9).

**Why:** Both amendments pass the two tests that killed FSRS (fold-time coefficients: nothing stored, retraction exact) — but passing the tests is not a reason to ship. They are the same class of speculative arithmetic as the dual bar: shaped for contested/cross-cutting data the corpus has not produced (no contested claim exists to tune against). Each is a one-line fold-time addition the day evidence arrives; until then every coefficient shipped untuned is a knob the gold set cannot yet ground.

**Prior art:** Hindsight (arXiv 2512.12818) and Knowledge Vault sqrt+Platt as the deferred shapes; DBSP retraction-correct aggregation; ADR-0012 §Costs (the named 'future confidence-weighted net').
### 11. What text does a claim's merge signature sign?

**Recommendation:** `claim.stmt_sig` derives from the SEED summary ∪ its corroborating events' summaries — a fold over LIVE edges — and NEVER from synthesized title/why prose.

**Why:** Evidence summaries are the register the matcher was measured on; Sonnet prose jumps register (concrete → general), and signing it chains transitive generalization — each merge widens the signature, which widens the next merge basin, the slow-motion version of the v2 failure. Folding over live edges also makes retraction shrink the basin automatically: tear out an event and its wording leaves the signature with it.

**Prior art:** ER practice of matching on source records, not on merged-entity descriptions (Senzing/Splink); §2.1's store-nothing-a-blob-implies discipline (ADR-0013).
### 12. What bounds the rough-claim pool, now that resolve consolidates every event?

**Recommendation:** A derived ACTIVE view: `active = recency-weighted entrenchment >= ACTIVE_FLOOR or evidence within ACTIVE_DAYS`. Inactive claims fold out of the subject/statement indexes and candidate generation; blobs, edges, and history remain; a re-seeded duplicate reconciles later via the derived merge-suggestion query at review.

**Why:** v2's pool was bounded by forget draining un-consolidated EVENTS — but v3 mints on every zero-match and consolidates everything, so forget never fires on the main path and the claim pool grows monotonically without a drain. A derived view keeps the bound while storing nothing (ADR-0013) and deleting nothing: dormancy is a query predicate, reversible by its nature, and the indexes it feeds are the only things that needed bounding (§3.3).

**Prior art:** MemGPT core-vs-archival (working set vs archive as a visibility boundary, not a deletion); DBSP view maintenance; dream v2's "small by design" pool argument, re-grounded for v3's mint-always behavior.
### 13. How do candidates surface at the similarities where real matches live?

**Recommendation:** An inverted index over each statement's K_RARE rarest shingles (rarity = document frequency over the active pool, recomputed per tick); a claim is a candidate at >= RARE_MIN shared rare shingles (K_RARE=8, RARE_MIN=2 — named, UNTUNED). SimHash-LSH band collision may remain as an exact-near-dup fast path, but it is not the recall channel.

**Why:** Measured same-lesson paraphrases sit at Jaccard 0.21–0.24, and the 4×16-bit LSH bands collide only for near-duplicates (J ≳ 0.6) — the LSH channel never surfaces the pairs the residue call exists to adjudicate. With the repo facet DF-devalued (correct on a single-repo corpus) and 47% of events carrying no writes, subject candidacy is thin too: without a statement channel that reaches 0.2-band similarities, most true pairs are never compared and the whole acceptance layer starves. Rare shingles are the cheapest thing that reaches: two tellings of one lesson share its unusual words even at low overall overlap; the index is a stdlib dict, O(active pool) to build like the others.

**Prior art:** Rare-token blocking / TF-IDF-weighted canopies (record-linkage practice: block on the discriminative tokens, not the common mass); Splink term-frequency adjustments; the measured band report as the forcing datum.

## Open questions

- **Residue-LLM verdict quality on ~10-word summaries — the load-bearing unknown.** On the measured
  corpus the residue call is the entire acceptance path, and nothing yet shows Haiku separates
  same-lesson from different-lesson at 0.2–0.35 better than the (measured-useless) Jaccard number.
  Gold-set validation via `sig --score-gold` is the instrument; ~60 stratified pairs await hand-labeling
  at `~/.local/share/ratchet/tuning/pairs_to_label.json`.
- **ACTIVE_FLOOR / ACTIVE_DAYS dormancy values** (§2.1): too tight and a slow-recurring lesson falls out
  of candidacy and re-seeds (recoverable via merge suggestions, but noisy); too loose and the view stops
  bounding the indexes.
- **Candidate/adjudication knob defaults** (§3.1): `K_RARE=8`/`RARE_MIN=2` are untuned — too strict
  strands paraphrases before the residue call ever sees them (the gold set's stranded-`same` count is
  the instrument); `K_RESIDUE=3` fits the measured residue (17 pairs corpus-wide), but a widened
